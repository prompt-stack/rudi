Two Approaches to Strategic AI Adoption

You can focus on technology or culture. RUDI focuses on people.

Cultural Axis (People-Centric)

  • Builds comfort by reducing fear and demystifying AI
  • Builds familiarity by embedding generative tools into daily work
  • Builds literacy through structured learning and governance frameworks

Focus: How people learn, adopt, and use AI responsibly

Technical Axis (System-Centric)

  • Aligns AI tools, data strategy, and infrastructure with organizational goals
  • Ensures compliance, transparency, and ethical standards
  • Modernizes performance, metrics, model impact, and ROI of AI initiatives

Focus: Infrastructure, compliance, and system-level optimization

RUDI Takes the People-Centered Approach

Five Proficiency Levels

Applied across each of the three dimensions

0

No Exposure

Conceptual: Never heard of generative AI • Operational: Never opened an AI tool • Governance: No awareness of AI risks

1

Pre-Beginner

Conceptual: Heard "AI" or "ChatGPT" but can't explain • Operational: Can open tools but unsure what to type • Governance: Knows AI may be biased but not how

2

Beginner

Conceptual: Can describe AI as "a tool that generates text/images" • Operational: Can try basic prompts, recognize when output is off • Governance: Can name risks like privacy, misinformation, bias

3

Intermediate

Conceptual: Understands training data, bias, why outputs vary • Operational: Uses AI for work tasks with cross-checking • Governance: Knows policies, applies them correctly

4

Advanced

Conceptual: Can critically discuss AI's societal role, ethics, limitations • Operational: Designs AI workflows, mentors peers • Governance: Leads conversations on AI policy, ethics, adoption

Most professionals start at Pre-Beginner or Beginner. RUDI training moves you systematically through each level across all three dimensions.

The Responsible Use Model

How to use AI safely and effectively in practice

Beyond technical competency, RUDI teaches responsible AI use through a three-dimensional model that answers: What data can I use? How carefully should I check outputs? What questions should I ask?

Habits of Responsible Use

The questions you ask

  • Source Tracing — Where did this come from?
  • Cross-Referencing — Does this hold up elsewhere?
  • Perspective Testing — Whose voice is present/missing?
  • Boundary Marking — What are the limits?
  • Bias Awareness — What assumptions shape this?

Levels of Scrutiny

How carefully to check

1

Light Use

Spell check, brainstorming, quick facts
Minimal scrutiny

2

Medium Use

Interpretation, analysis, abstract problems
Apply 2-3 habits

3

High-Stakes Use

Legal, medical, financial decisions
Apply ALL 5 habits + verify with experts

Data Types

What you're allowed to use

Public Data

Open-source info, articles, brainstorming

Internal Data

Workflows, non-sensitive reports (check policy)

Sensitive Data

PII, HR records (must anonymize first)

Prohibited Data

Protected records, trade secrets (never)

This three-dimensional model ensures safe, effective AI use. RUDI training builds these habits into your daily workflow.
